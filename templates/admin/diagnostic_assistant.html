Common HVAC Issues & Troubleshooting

Not Cooling or Cooling Poorly

+

Check system operation status first, then follow the appropriate path below.

Outdoor Unit Running - Low Cooling Output

+
  1. Dirty or Restricted Evaporator Coil

    Symptoms: Low airflow at registers, high indoor humidity. Solution: Check filter condition and coil visibly for dust/pet hair. Replace filter and clean coil if necessary.

  2. Low Refrigerant Charge

    Symptoms: Suction line cold/frosted, system running but weak cooling, high suction pressure. Solution: Check for visible leaks, measure superheat against outdoor temperature, verify charge before adding refrigerant.

  3. Dirty Condenser Coil (Outdoor Unit)

    Symptoms: Hot liquid line, high head pressure, compressor running hot. Solution: Hose off condenser coil from top down. Avoid high-pressure washers that damage fins.

  4. Weak Airflow from Indoor Blower

    Symptoms: All registers blowing weak, thermostat shows cool but you don't feel it. Solution: Check filter, verify blower running at correct speed tap, check for ductwork restrictions.

  5. Thermostat Set Incorrectly

    Symptoms: System runs but setpoint too high. Solution: Verify mode is set to Cool and setpoint is below actual temperature. Check for programming errors.

Outdoor Unit Not Running

+
  1. No Power to Outdoor Unit

    Symptoms: Outdoor unit completely silent. Solution: Check disconnect switch, breaker, and 240V at disconnect with meter. Verify wire connections at breaker.

  2. Thermostat Not Calling for Cool

    Symptoms: Indoor fan running but outdoor unit silent. Solution: Check thermostat Y terminal for 24V call. Verify thermostat mode and setpoint.

  3. Contactor Not Pulling In

    Symptoms: Hum from outdoor unit but fan not running. Solution: Check 24V at contactor coil, measure coil resistance. Check for pitted contacts.

  4. Capacitor Failed (Outdoor)

    Symptoms: Compressor hums but doesn't start, or fan won't turn. Solution: Check capacitor microfarads with meter, compare to nameplate. Replace if out of range.

  5. Compressor Motor Protection Tripped

    Symptoms: Compressor not running after previous short cycles. Solution: Check compressor amps during start, look for thermal overload trips. Verify voltage at motor terminals.

Not Heating or Heating Poorly

+

Follow the ignition sequence and check safety circuits systematically.

Furnace Running - Weak Heat Output

+
  1. Restricted Airflow / Dirty Filter

    Symptoms: Low temperature rise across furnace, weak airflow at registers. Solution: Replace filter, check blower speed tap, verify no ductwork obstructions.

  2. Low Gas Pressure / Weak Flame

    Symptoms: Furnace cycles off early, incomplete combustion visible. Solution: Check gas pressure at furnace regulator against specs. Verify gas valve opens fully under call.

  3. Heat Exchanger Blocked or Cracked

    Symptoms: Temperature rise too low even with good gas, soot buildup visible. Solution: Inspect heat exchanger visibly for cracks or carbon blockage. May require replacement.

  4. Blower Running at Wrong Speed

    Symptoms: Furnace hot but weak airflow. Solution: Check blower speed tap setting against furnace nameplate for heating mode. Verify capacitor condition.

  5. Thermostat Setpoint or Mode Wrong

    Symptoms: System runs but seems weak. Solution: Verify thermostat set to Heat mode, setpoint above current temp, and G terminal calls blower.

Furnace Not Igniting

+
  1. Pressure Switch Not Closing (Inducer Not Running)

    Symptoms: Control board not energizing rest of sequence, lockout after 3 attempts. Solution: Listen for inducer motor, check for venting blockage, verify pressure switch tubing connected.

  2. Igniter Failed (Hot Surface or Spark)

    Symptoms: Board energizes gas valve but no flame appears. Solution: Check igniter for visible cracks or glow if hot surface type. Measure continuity if spark type.

  3. Gas Valve Not Opening

    Symptoms: Smell gas but no flame. Solution: Verify 24V at gas valve terminals during call, check valve coil resistance. Ensure manual gas shutoff is on.

  4. Flame Rectification Too Weak

    Symptoms: Flame ignites briefly then lockout occurs. Solution: Inspect flame shape and color, check for soot on flame sensor, verify microamp reading during operation.

  5. Rollout Switch Open (Safety Trip)

    Symptoms: Furnace starts then stops immediately. Solution: Verify furnace area clear of obstructions, check venting for blockage, measure combustion air supply.

System Not Turning On At All

+

Start with power verification and work through the control chain.

  1. No Power to System

    Symptoms: No lights on controls, no sounds. Solution: Check main disconnect, breaker panel, measure 240V at disconnect with meter. Check 24V transformer output.

  2. Thermostat Not Communicating

    Symptoms: Thermostat has power but equipment won't respond. Solution: Check 24V at thermostat, verify G/C wiring, test Y or W terminal voltage during call.

  3. Control Board Failure

    Symptoms: Power present but no relay clicks when thermostat calls. Solution: Check for blown fuses on board, burned components, measure relay coil voltage.

  4. Main Gas Shutoff or Oil Valve Closed

    Symptoms: Equipment tries to run but lockout occurs (heating mode). Solution: Verify manual gas shutoff is fully open, check burner oil supply valve if oil-fired.

  5. Safety Switch Open (Limit, Pressure, Rollout)

    Symptoms: Blower tries to run but stops immediately. Solution: Check all safety switch wiring and tubing, verify furnace doors/panels fully closed, inspect venting.

System Runs Constantly & Won't Shut Off

+

Check thermostat settings and control logic first.

  1. Thermostat Set to "Fan Always On"

    Symptoms: Blower runs constantly regardless of temperature. Solution: Check thermostat fan setting, change from "On" to "Auto" if appropriate.

  2. Setpoint Set Continuously Demanding

    Symptoms: In heating mode, heat target unreachably high; in cooling, target unreachably low. Solution: Verify setpoint is realistic and achievable. Check if programming schedule is correct.

  3. Temperature Sensor Reads Incorrectly

    Symptoms: Thermostat displays wrong temperature. Solution: Verify sensor is not in draft or direct sunlight, check thermostat calibration, compare displayed temp to accurate thermometer.

  4. Contactor Stuck Closed (Cooling/Heating Contactor)

    Symptoms: Equipment runs and won't turn off even when setpoint reached. Solution: Verify 24V at thermostat W/Y terminal goes to zero when satisfied, check contactor coil de-energizes, tap stuck contactor if stuck.

  5. Limit or Pressure Switch Failure

    Symptoms: Blower continues running past normal run time in heating. Solution: Check furnace high-limit switch with multimeter during operation, verify pressure switches for sticking or electrical failure.

Strange Noises or Smells

+

Isolate the source and determine if equipment is safe to operate.

Squealing, Grinding, or Rattling Sounds

+
  1. Blower Wheel Loose or Hitting Housing

    Symptoms: Grinding or scraping noise when blower runs. Solution: Shut off power, inspect blower wheel for debris or looseness, tighten motor mounting, check for wheel rub.

  2. Motor Bearing Worn or Lacking Lubrication

    Symptoms: Squealing noise from blower or compressor. Solution: Check for oil ports on motor, verify capacitor operation, measure motor amps. May indicate bearing replacement needed.

  3. Ductwork or Sheet Metal Loose

    Symptoms: Rattling or buzzing from walls/vents. Solution: Locate source by listening, tighten ductwork connections, check for loose hangers or insulation contact.

  4. Compressor Internal Damage

    Symptoms: Loud grinding from outdoor unit during operation. Solution: Listen carefully to compressor vs other components, verify suction/discharge line temps normal. May require compressor replacement.

  5. Refrigerant Floodback or Liquid Slugging

    Symptoms: Loud bang or clank when compressor shuts down. Solution: Check suction line insulation, measure superheat, ensure proper expansion device operation.

Burning, Chemical, or Fuel Smells

+
  1. First Startup Dust Burn-off

    Symptoms: Smell when system first runs after long shutdown. Solution: Run system for 15-20 minutes with windows open. If smell continues after extended operation, investigate further.

  2. Motor Winding Overheating

    Symptoms: Sharp plastic/insulation burn smell from blower or outdoor compressor. Solution: Measure motor amps, verify voltage correct, check for restricted airflow. Shut down if smell strong.

  3. Gas Leak (Furnace/Boiler)

    Symptoms: Sulfur or rotten egg smell. Solution: EVACUATE SPACE. Use gas detector, do not use ignition sources. Call utility company gas leak line immediately.

  4. Dirty or Blocked Heat Exchanger

    Symptoms: Burning smell during heating, possible soot smell. Solution: Inspect heat exchanger for carbon buildup, verify combustion air flow, may need professional cleaning or replacement.

  5. Electrical Component Failure

    Symptoms: Acrid electric smell, possible visible burned component. Solution: Power down immediately, visually inspect board for burned components, check for loose wire connections causing arcing.

Water Leak or Ice Buildup

+

Water issues usually indicate airflow or condensate drainage problems.

  1. Condensate Drain Line Blocked or Disconnected

    Symptoms: Water pooling under indoor unit. Solution: Check drain line for kinks or blockage, verify connection at unit and termination point, flush with wet vac if blocked with algae.

  2. Low Refrigerant Charge (Evaporator Freezing)

    Symptoms: Ice on suction line, freeze-up of coil and water overflow. Solution: Measure superheat, verify refrigerant quantity, check for leaks and repair before adding charge.

  3. Restricted Airflow Over Evaporator Coil

    Symptoms: Coil ices up even with normal charge. Solution: Check filter condition, verify blower speed correct, inspect coil for dust/pet hair, check for supply duct leaks before coil.

  4. Thermostat Set Below Outdoor Dew Point in Dry Climate

    Symptoms: Excessive condensation forming. Solution: Adjust setpoint to realistic level, verify humidity levels, check if humidifier is on in heating season.

  5. Coil Sloped Incorrectly or Pan Cracked

    Symptoms: Water leaking instead of draining, even with clear drain line. Solution: Check unit is level, inspect pan for cracks or corrosion, verify drain line slope gravity-fed toward exit.

High Energy Bill

+

Compare historical usage and check for system inefficiencies.

  1. Excessive Short Cycling

    Symptoms: System runs for short periods frequently. Solution: Check thermostat differential/deadband setting, verify system sized correctly, examine thermostat placement for drafts or sunlight.

  2. Dirty Filter or Coils

    Symptoms: System running longer than normal to reach setpoint. Solution: Replace filter, clean evaporator and condenser coils, verify airflow matches design CFM.

  3. Ductwork Leakage

    Symptoms: Uneven temperatures room to room, some vents blowing weak. Solution: Visually inspect ducts in attic/crawl for disconnected joints, holes, or poor insulation. Seal and insulate.

  4. Incorrect Thermostat Schedule or Settings

    Symptoms: Heating/cooling running when not needed, setpoint unrealistic. Solution: Review thermostat programming, check if in continuous mode (should be auto), verify schedule matches occupancy.

  5. Low Refrigerant or Undercharged System

    Symptoms: Compressor running longer to satisfy cooling call. Solution: Measure superheat at various outdoor temperatures, check for leaks, verify correct charge according to nameplate or txv design.

Uneven Temperatures Room to Room

+

Investigate airflow distribution and zoning control.

  1. Ductwork Restrictions or Leaks

    Symptoms: Some rooms cold/hot while others are comfortable. Solution: Feel air at different supply vents and returns, locate blockages (furniture, damper, collapsed duct), verify connections tight.

  2. Supply Dampers Partially Closed

    Symptoms: One or more rooms not getting air. Solution: Check duct dampers in attic/basement, verify all damper handles are open position, ensure zone control system operates correctly.

  3. Return Air Blockage or Imbalance

    Symptoms: Rooms far from return grill uncomfortable. Solution: Verify return pathways clear (undercut doors, open returns), check filter not dirty, measure static pressure.

  4. Thermostat Placement Poor

    Symptoms: Thermostat in hallway but bedrooms different temp. Solution: Check for drafts, sunlight, or heat sources near thermostat affecting reading. Consider remote sensors if zoning available.

  5. Blower Speed Too Low for System

    Symptoms: Weak airflow throughout. Solution: Verify blower speed tap correct for heating/cooling mode, check capacitor condition, measure static pressure against design specs.
